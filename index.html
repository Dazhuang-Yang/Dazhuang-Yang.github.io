Neural-based Pose Estimation and 3D Reconstruction for Non-Cooperative Maneuvering Space Target

Dazhuang Yang, Jiafu Jiao, Yizhai Zhang, Panfeng Huang
Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi'an, China
Paper Code Dataset

Abstract

Accurate pose estimation and 3D reconstruction of Non-Cooperative Space Targets (NCST) are critical for proximity operations in active debris removal and on-orbit servicing. However, existing methods often rely on point-based representations, which face challenges in efficiency and reliability under sparse textures and dynamic conditions. This paper proposes a novel NeRF-based Simultaneous Pose Estimation and 3D Reconstruction (SPAR) to address these challenges. The framework integrates three key components: a multi-resolution hash encoder to reduce computational cost, pre-trained image features to enhance view generalization, and a direct method to stabilize pose estimation. Evaluations on a new Spacecraft Pose Estimation and 3D Reconstruction Dataset (SPARD), comprising both synthetic and real RGB-D images, demonstrate the framework's effectiveness. The framework achieving real-time processing at 51 Hz with a pose estimation accuracy of 1.26 cm translation error and 0.97Â° rotation error. The results show improvements over traditional and NeRF-based baselines, validating its applicability to space missions with NCST.
